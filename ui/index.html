    Understanding Bursts & Clusters

    üì∏ Bursts

    A burst is a group of photos taken in rapid succession (within 700ms of each other by default). For example, when you hold down the shutter button or use burst mode, all those photos form one burst. Bursts are detected by analyzing the timestamp gaps between photos.

    üéØ Clusters

    A cluster is a group of similar or near-duplicate images found anywhere in your photo set. Images are clustered using perceptual hashing - photos that look similar (same scene, angle, composition) are grouped together, even if they're from different bursts or taken at different times.

    Example:

    You take 10 burst shots of a group photo, then 5 more bursts later from a slightly different angle. This creates 2 bursts (based on time gaps) but might result in just 1 cluster (all 15 photos are similar enough to be grouped together). PhotoCull selects the best photo from each cluster as the winner.

    üèÜ Winners

    The best photo from each cluster based on: sharpness, open eyes, good exposure, no motion blur, and aesthetic composition.
